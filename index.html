Adult Physical Exam (Brief)

General: The patient is alert and oriented and in no apparent distress
HEENT: Pupils are equal and reactive. Extraocular movement intact. Nasal mucosa not inflamed. Oropharynx non-erythematous and without exudate.
Neck: Supple without lymphadenopathy. Thyroid non-palpable.
Heart: Regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs, rubs, or gallups.
Lungs: Clear to auscultation bilaterally. No wheezes, rales, or rhonchi.
Abdomen: Soft, nontender, nondistended. Positive bowel sounds. Bruits absent.
Extremities: Warm and well-perfused. No cyanosis, clubbing or edema.
Neuro: Grossly nonfocal.
Skin: No lesions or rashes.

Neonatal Exam

General: active infant in no apparent distress
Head: normocephalic, fontanelles and suture lines within normal limits. Hair full and thick.
Eyes: Sclera clear, cornea and lens clear, PERRL, red reflex present bilaterally
Ears: symmetrical, without malformations
Nose: Mucosa pink without lesions or discharge, septum midline, airway patent.
Oropharynx: Mucosa moist without lesion. Midline uvula, pharynx non-erythematous, hard and soft palate intact.
Neck: No sinus tracts, no lymphadenopathy.
Chest: Clear to auscultation bilaterally.
CV: regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs, heaves or thrills. Radial and femoral pulses present bilaterally.
Abdomen: Umbilical cord with 3 vessels and clamp in place. No hepatosplenomegaly, no masses. Kidneys non-palpable. No hernias palpated.
Extremities: Symmetrical without deformities. Ortalani and Barlow maneuvers negative. Normal muscle tone. Bilateral FROM. No cyanosis or clubbing.
Neuro: Root, suck, and Moro reflexes intact.
Genitalia: Anus patent. Comments on (female) presence of hymen or (male) foreskin presence, urethral orifice location, and location of testes.
